Clinical Operations Summary Report

This document summarizes outpatient care patterns and follow-up risks in a small medical operations setting.

Summary
- Patients with diabetes and hypertension appeared frequently in follow-up scheduling records.
- Common concerns included medication adherence, blood pressure control, glucose monitoring, and delayed revisit dates.
- Older patients with multiple chronic conditions were more likely to require repeated follow-up.
- Several notes suggested that missed appointments increased the risk of worsening symptom control.

Observations
1. Follow-up timing matters because delayed revisits can lead to unstable chronic disease management.
2. Medication compliance is a recurring theme in both consultation notes and care planning.
3. Patients with combined chronic conditions often need more detailed monitoring plans.
4. A useful RAG pipeline should retrieve both narrative notes and structured patient records.
